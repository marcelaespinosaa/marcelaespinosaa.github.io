Notifications

A platform-wide notification problem hiding in plain sight — and a research-driven process that shaped the infrastructure decision powering Drata's next phase of growth.

Senior Product Designer
Group Product Manager, Staff Engineer
Overview

A platform-wide notification system that had outgrown its infrastructure.

Notifications play a critical role in Drata's product experience. They inform users about compliance tasks, system alerts, vendor reviews, trust center access requests, and collaboration activity across the platform.

As the platform expanded, notifications were becoming increasingly complex to manage and scale. Different product areas generated notifications in different ways, upcoming initiatives were expected to significantly increase volume, and there was no unified product strategy to guide how notifications should behave across the platform.

I led the UX strategy for this initiative, alongside a Group Product Manager — structuring the product requirements, defining representative notification workflows, and grounding a major infrastructure decision in user research and experience design principles.

The result: a structured UX-driven evaluation that shaped the team's decision on the notification infrastructure powering Drata's next phase of growth.

My Role

Owning the UX strategy that turned fragmented insights into a clear infrastructure path.

I partnered closely with a Group Product Manager leading the initiative and a Staff Software Engineer responsible for evaluating technical feasibility.

My role owned the product and user experience side of the initiative end-to-end, including:

  • Synthesizing customer feedback and internal product insights related to notifications
  • Defining representative notification workflows to clarify product requirements
  • Identifying usability gaps and opportunities in the current notification experience
  • Evaluating how infrastructure platforms could support future notification needs
  • Contributing to the recommendation for the notification platform best suited for Drata's product
Impact

A research-driven decision that will shape how Drata notifies its users for years.

This work drove the team's decision to transition Drata's notification infrastructure to a new provider — one better aligned with the platform's product requirements and long-term growth.

The Problem

Notifications had grown without a strategy — and it was starting to show.

Notifications were embedded across many workflows in the platform but had evolved without a cohesive product strategy.

As a result:

  • Notifications behaved inconsistently across product features
  • Users had limited control over notification preferences
  • Important alerts could be difficult to distinguish from routine system messages
  • Engineering teams struggled to scale notification logic as the product grew

At the same time, upcoming product initiatives were expected to increase notification complexity and volume.

To support this growth, the team needed to better understand how notifications were used across the platform and define the capabilities required from a notification infrastructure provider.

Research & Insight Synthesis

Months of scattered feedback, organized into a clear picture.

I synthesized customer feedback and internal product insights that had been gathered over several months related to notifications.

This included:

  • Productboard feedback from customers
  • Insights from prior research conversations
  • Internal discussions across product and support teams

I organized the findings into thematic clusters to identify recurring user pain points.

Defining Notification System Requirements

Mapping what the system needed to support — not just what it did.

Notifications are triggered across many different workflows in the platform, but the existing infrastructure did not support several scenarios the product team anticipated needing in the future.

To clarify these needs, I worked with the Group Product Manager to define representative workflows that illustrated the types of notification behaviors the platform would need to support.

These workflows were not meant to document the current system, but rather to define product requirements and highlight gaps in the existing notification infrastructure.

Examples included scenarios such as:

  • Users being assigned tasks within compliance workflows
  • Critical control failures that require immediate attention
  • Trust center access requests from external stakeholders
  • Collaboration events between users working on the same tasks

These representative workflows helped us communicate concrete notification requirements when evaluating infrastructure providers.

They also exposed several limitations in the existing system, particularly around consistency, flexibility, and the ability to support more complex notification logic.

Collaboration & Interaction Exploration

Understanding how notifications and collaboration shaped each other.

During the analysis, we also explored how notifications intersected with collaboration workflows.

This included evaluating interaction patterns such as:

  • Internal notes and comments
  • User mentions
  • Threaded communication
  • Shared context between users working on compliance tasks

Platform Evaluation

Testing infrastructure options against real product requirements.

Once these requirements were defined, the Group Product Manager, Staff Engineer, and I evaluated notification infrastructure platforms capable of supporting those needs.

The evaluation focused on:

Multi-channel support

Support for email, Slack, Teams, and future notification channels

Scalability

Scalability for increasing notification volume as the platform grows

Configuration flexibility

Configuration flexibility for engineering teams building notification logic

White-label support

Support for white-labeled notifications to align with customer branding needs

Engineering evaluated integration complexity while product and design assessed how well each platform could support the workflows and requirements defined during the research phase.

Decision & Recommendation

The right call, grounded in both technical and experience criteria.

Based on the evaluation, the team recommended transitioning to a new notification infrastructure provider better aligned with Drata's product and engineering needs.

The decision was informed by both technical capabilities and the product requirements identified through research and workflow analysis.

Because the existing implementation used only a limited subset of the previous platform's functionality, the engineering effort required to migrate to a new system was considered manageable.

Reflection

Infrastructure decisions are product decisions — and design has a role in both.

This project highlighted how deeply notifications are embedded in product workflows and how difficult they become to scale without a clear infrastructure strategy.

By grounding the evaluation process in research and workflow analysis, the team was able to make a more informed infrastructure decision that considered both technical capabilities and user experience needs.

The work also reinforced the importance of aligning infrastructure decisions with long-term product experience goals as the platform continues to evolve.
